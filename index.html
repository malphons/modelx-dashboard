Approach

① Exposure Decomposition

A weighted regression decomposes each observed price into its key drivers — equipment category, manufacturer brand premium, physical condition, and asset age. Data sources are weighted by reliability so that closed transactions anchor the model while indicative prices provide coverage.

② Trend Extraction

A proprietary smoothing layer separates genuine price movement from observation noise across irregularly-spaced data. The method adapts its sensitivity — placing more trust in recent, well-observed periods while stabilising estimates where data is sparse.

③ Fair Value Estimation

State estimation techniques produce a final fair market value for each asset category, complete with 95% confidence intervals. The model continuously tracks both the price level and its momentum, updating as new market observations arrive.

Data Quality Assessment
Liquidity & Richness Analysis
Not all inventory is equally modelable. We score each category across six dimensions of data richness to identify where the model has the strongest statistical foundation — and where additional data acquisition would have the highest ROI.
📊 Observation Depth

How many comparable transactions exist? Categories with deeper pools yield more stable estimates and tighter confidence intervals. We measure this on a log-scale because doubling from 100 to 200 obs matters more than 1,000 to 1,100.

🔀 Source Diversity

Do we observe prices from multiple channels — auctions, dealer listings, closed transactions? Categories seen from only one source may carry systematic bias. Shannon entropy quantifies how evenly price discovery is spread across channels.

📅 Temporal Coverage

Are observations spread across time, or clustered in a single batch? Dense temporal coverage lets the model detect trends and seasonality. Sparse time-series get wider confidence bands because we are extrapolating between observations.

📈 Price Coherence

Do similar assets trade at similar prices? A high signal-to-noise ratio means the category has an identifiable "fair value" the model can latch onto. Low coherence may indicate the category lumps together heterogeneous equipment.

🏭 Manufacturer Mix

Is the category dominated by one OEM, or spread across many brands? A diversified manufacturer mix means brand effects can be estimated reliably — critical for understanding why a Caterpillar commands a premium over a lesser-known brand.

🔧 Condition Spectrum

Do we see new, used, and refurbished units? Full condition coverage lets the model estimate the depreciation curve. Categories where everything is listed as "unknown" condition lose this pricing lever entirely.
